## What we DEFERRED, and why

The plan called for enabling `cacheComponents: true` globally and adding `'use cache'` directives across the app. After auditing the codebase against the Next 16 docs, this is a substantially larger lift than V2.2's 2-day slot allows:

- `cacheComponents: true` puts the renderer into the **new model**: any component that reads runtime APIs (`cookies`, `headers`, `searchParams`, `params`) or does an uncached async fetch MUST be wrapped in `<Suspense>` or marked `'use cache'`. The previous default (everything dynamic) becomes an error.
- Every authed page in this app reads `cookies` indirectly via the Supabase server client (`lib/supabase/server.ts`). That's ~30+ pages across `/dashboard`, `/supplier`, `/admin`.
- A full adoption requires:
  1. Auditing every route to determine cache vs. dynamic
  2. Adding `<Suspense>` boundaries around dynamic regions
  3. Adding `'use cache'` to genuinely cacheable units (marketing pages, blog, public discover list)
  4. Verifying no regression in the auth-gated flows
- Estimate: 5â€“7 working days of dedicated effort, not the 2 days slotted in the plan.

**Decision:** ship the tag-invalidation win now (low-risk, immediate value for marketing pages), defer the full `cacheComponents` adoption to its own sprint where it gets the audit attention it needs.
